**Third-Party** **Website** **Audit** **Report**

**Subject:** Independent Audit of
[*<u>https://trivexa-five.vercel.app/</u>*](https://trivexa-five.vercel.app/)
**Date:** 22 September 2025

**Prepared** **by:** Independent Digital Audit Consultant

**1.** **Executive** **Summary**

This audit evaluates the website
<u>[*https://trivexa-five.vercel.app/*](https://trivexa-five.vercel.app/),</u>
which positions itself as a blockchain and Web3 enterprise solutions
provider under the brand *Trivexa* *Technologies* *Pvt.* *Ltd.*. The
assessment covers **credibility,** **trustworthiness,** **technical**
**robustness,** **security,** **compliance,** **and** **SEO/UX**
**performance**.

**Overall** **rating:** *Medium–High* *Risk* */* *Requires*
*Remediation*

The site demonstrates a modern presentation but suffers from critical
gaps in **credibility** **(placeholders,** **unverifiable** **metrics,**
**hosting** **on** **a** **Vercel** **subdomain)** and **compliance**
**(missing** **legal/registration** **evidence,** **privacy**
**terms)**. Unless corrected, these issues may reduce trust with
investors, clients, and regulators.

**2.** **Key** **Observations**

**2.1** **Hosting** **&** **Domain**

> ● The site is hosted on a **Vercel** **subdomain**
> (trivexa-five.vercel.app).
>
> ● No evidence of deployment under a **corporate** **domain** (e.g.,
> trivexa.com).
>
> ● Subdomain hosting reduces professional credibility and creates
> uncertainty about ownership authenticity.

**Recommendation:** Acquire and host on an official domain, with
DNS/WHOIS showing the company entity.

**2.2** **Corporate** **Identity** **&** **Legal** **Compliance**

> ● Footer states: *©* *2025* *Trivexa* *Technologies* *Pvt.* *Ltd.*
>
> ● Contact details include **+91-6301846681** and
> **hello@trivexa.com**.
>
> ● No verifiable company registration (CIN, GST, UEN, or equivalent) is
> displayed.
>
> ● Privacy Policy, Terms & Conditions, and Cookies Policy are listed
> but not fully accessible.

**Recommendation:**

> ● Display verified legal entity details (CIN/GST for India or UEN for
> Singapore).
>
> ● Ensure compliance documents (Privacy, Terms, Cookies) are complete
> and functional.

**2.3** **Portfolio** **&** **Case** **Studies**

> ● Portfolio pages show impressive but **unverified** **metrics**
> (e.g., \$50M+ TVL, 10k+ users, A+ security score).
>
> ● **Client** **names** **appear** **as** **placeholders** (Client
> 1–6).
>
> ● References to “View Code / Live Demo” exist but do not provide
> working external links.

**Observation:** These appear **template-based,** **not**
**client-verified**.

**Recommendation:** Replace placeholders with **verifiable** **client**
**references**, working demo links, or GitHub repos.

**2.4** **Content** **&** **Blog**

> ● Blog posts dated January 2024 are displayed, with author names and
> structured layouts. ● However, originality and external reach could
> not be independently confirmed.

**Recommendation:**

> ● Publish original insights backed by industry expertise.
>
> ● Cross-post content on LinkedIn/Medium to establish authorship
> credibility.

**2.5** **Security** **&** **Transparency**

> ● The company positions itself as a **blockchain/DeFi** **developer**,
> yet no public audit reports are cited.
>
> ● Absence of links to third-party security audits (e.g., CertiK,
> OpenZeppelin). ● No evidence of bug bounty programs or penetration
> testing.

**Recommendation:**

> ● Commission a **formal** **smart** **contract** **security**
> **audit** and publish the reports.
>
> ● Add visible trust seals (audit certificates, ISO/IEC compliance, SSL
> security statements).

**2.6** **Technical** **&** **SEO** **Audit**

> ● Built on **Next.js** **with** **Vercel** **deployment** (modern
> stack).
>
> ● Missing SEO essentials: sitemap.xml, schema markup, canonical tags.
>
> ● Accessibility compliance is incomplete (alt attributes, ARIA roles
> missing).

**Recommendation:**

> ● Implement SEO fundamentals (sitemap, robots.txt, schema).
>
> ● Improve accessibility (WCAG compliance, alt text, semantic
> structure).

**4.** **Recommendations** **for** **Remediation**

> 1\. **Domain** **&** **Identity**
>
> ○ Migrate site to *trivexa.com* (or equivalent corporate domain).
>
> ○ Publish company registration details (CIN/UEN, GST, legal address).
> 2. **Portfolio** **Transparency**
>
> ○ Replace placeholder client case studies with verified references. ○
> Provide links to live projects or public code repositories.
>
> 3\. **Security** **Assurance**
>
> ○ Publish smart contract audit reports by recognized firms.
>
> ○ Create a transparency page for compliance, security certifications,
> and processes. 4. **Compliance** **Documentation**
>
> ○ Finalize and display accessible Privacy Policy, Terms, and Cookie
> Policy.
>
> 5\. **SEO** **&** **Accessibility**
>
> ○ Add sitemap.xml, structured data, canonical URLs. ○ Ensure WCAG 2.1
> compliance for accessibility.

**5.** **Conclusion**

The website presents a **modern,** **professional** **design** but has
**critical** **credibility** **and** **compliance** **gaps**. To attract
enterprise clients, investors, and regulators, Trivexa must immediately:

> ● Move to an official domain.
>
> ● Publish verifiable company details and client references.
>
> ● Provide transparent security audits and compliance documentation.

**Without** **these** **corrections,** **stakeholders** **may**
**perceive** **the** **company** **as** **unverified** **or**
**high-risk.**
